Why Choose Sewer Camera Inspections?

Sewer camera inspections bring cutting-edge technology to your doorstep without invasive procedures. By utilizing high-resolution cameras, our specialists can precisely pinpoint issues such as:

Frequently Asked Questions

A sewer camera inspection involves using a specialized waterproof camera to visually inspect the interior of sewer lines and other underground pipes, including those under your home's foundation. This service is typically performed by plumbing professionals to identify any potential blockages, breakages, or other issues.

A sewer camera inspection helps identify issues such as clogs, leaks, root intrusions, and structural problems within the pipes that are not visible from the surface. Early detection of these issues can prevent more significant and costly repairs down the road.

The frequency of sewer camera inspections can vary. For residential homes, it's typically recommended to perform an inspection every 1-2 years. However, older homes or properties with frequent drainage issues may benefit from more frequent inspections.

Common signs that you may need a sewer camera inspection include frequent drain clogs, unexplained foul odors, slow drainage, irregular toilet water levels, and unexpected spikes in your water bill. These signs could indicate underlying sewer line issues.

No, a sewer camera inspection is non-invasive. It involves inserting a small, flexible camera into the pipes through an existing access point or cleanout. This allows plumbers to inspect the pipes without the need for any digging or destruction of property.
