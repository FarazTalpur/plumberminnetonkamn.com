Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs include strange noises, irregular cycling, the sump pump not turning on, water pooling around the pump, and it continuously running without stopping.

It's recommended to test your sump pump at least once every three months. You should also perform a more thorough inspection and test before the rainy season or if you've experienced any power outages.

Potential causes include power failure, a stuck or faulty float switch, clogged discharge pipes, motor failure, and it being unplugged accidentally. Check these factors if the pump stops working.

Simple issues like replacing the float switch or clearing a clogged pipe can often be done by a homeowner with basic tools. However, for electrical problems or motor issues, it's best to call a professional.

A standard sump pump usually lasts about 7 to 10 years. Regular maintenance can extend its life, but after this period, it's wise to consider a replacement to prevent unexpected failures.
